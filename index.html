I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the Université de Montréal. My primary areas of research are Econometrics, Macroeconomics, Machine Learning, Climate Finance, and Macro-Financial Issues.

I previously worked in the IMF’s Research Department on debt sustainability, fiscal and monetary policies, and climate change and inequality. I hold a Master’s degree in Economics from the University College London (UCL) and a Bachelor’s degree in Economics from the Nova School of Business and Economics.

In summer 2021 I have been working on carbon pricing at the Research Department of the International Monetary Fund. Before starting my PhD, I worked several years for the thinktank adelphi, designing, coordinating, and contributing to research and consultancy projects on a variety of environmental topics. Having a multidisciplinary background I aim to build bridges between the natural and social sciences. I am also a passionate teacher of economics and environmental sciences. I have been designing and teaching courses on environmental economics, development and trade, sustainable development, and environmental change. Research interests: • Environmental economics • Economics of climate change • Climate econometrics • Development economics • Economic geography I am co-organising the Shifting Landscapes 2022 conference for PhD students and early-career researchers.
