Welcome to my website! I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Montreal.


My research interests include Econometrics, Big Data Techniques, Causal Inference, Machine Learning/Textual Analysis, Household Finance and Climate Finance.


My research focuses on factor analysis in high-dimensional datasets and large panel models with interactive fixed effects. I am also interested in applying Machine Learning (ML) and Natural Language Processing (NLP) methods to answer macro-finance, and climate finance questions. My teaching interests are quantitative methods for economics and business, machine learning, climate finance, and open-source programming (i.e., R/Python).


During the summer of 2021, I joined the International Monetary Fund as a Ph.D. intern in the Fund Internship Program (FIP). I was an Economist consultant at the Observatory of the Economic Francophonie during the fall of 2020. I also previously worked as an Economist in the IMF’s Senegal Country Desk and the Macroeconomics Trade Investment (MTI) of the World Bank.


Prior to this, I hold a master’s degree in Statistics and Economics from ENSAE.
